# Galaxy Simulator - Quick Start Guide

## Welcome to the Galaxy Simulator!

The Galaxy Simulator generates infinite worlds with thousands of years of interconnected history. Each galaxy features pre-computed civilizations, wars, discoveries, and cultural evolution—all emerging from a causal system where events cascade realistically through time.

## Getting Started in 3 Steps

### 1. Launch the Application

Navigate to the home page (/) and you'll see the **Galaxy Launcher**—a beautiful one-click interface for generating new worlds.

```
https://your-domain.com/
```

### 2. Choose a Preset or Create Custom

The Launcher offers four preset configurations:

| Preset | Species | Duration | Narrative Depth | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|-----------------|----------|
| **Quick Start** ⚡ | 3 | 5,000 years | Medium | First-time users, quick exploration |
| **Epic Saga** 🏰 | 5 | 50,000 years | Deep | Detailed histories, multiple civilizations |
| **Intimate Story** 💫 | 2 | 10,000 years | Deep | Focused narratives, deep character development |
| **Vast Cosmos** 🌌 | 8 | 100,000 years | Medium | Maximum complexity, exploration |

**To generate a galaxy:**
1. Click on a preset card to select it
2. Enter a name for your galaxy (e.g., "The Mycelial Realms")
3. Click "Generate Galaxy"
4. Watch the progress bar as the simulation pre-computes thousands of years of history
5. Once complete, you'll be taken to the galaxy detail view

### 3. Explore Your Galaxy

Once generated, you can explore:

- **Chronicle**: Read the chronological history of all events
- **Timeline**: Visualize events across time with filtering
- **Species Encyclopedia**: Learn about each species' traits, achievements, and fate
- **Genealogy**: Trace family lineages and leadership succession
- **Figures**: Discover important historical figures and their influence
- **Causal Chains**: See how events connect and influence each other

## Understanding the Simulation

### What Gets Generated?

Each galaxy includes:

**Civilizations:**
- Multiple species with unique traits (aquatic, isolationist, religious, etc.)
- Technological progression from stone age through space age
- Cultural values and philosophical traditions
- Wars, alliances, and trade networks

**Events:**
- Major discoveries and technological breakthroughs
- Wars and conflicts with cascading consequences
- First contact between civilizations
- Natural disasters and environmental changes
- Cultural shifts and philosophical movements
- Individual historical figures and their achievements

**Interconnections:**
- Trade networks spreading resources and ideas
- Wars causing migrations and territorial changes
- Discoveries enabling new technologies
- Environmental changes forcing cultural adaptation
- Ripple effects from major events propagating through time

### How Does It Work?

The simulator uses a **causal graph** where events aren't randomly generated—they emerge from civilization state:

1. **Significance Scoring**: Not every event gets logged. The system identifies truly important moments (wars, extinctions, discoveries) versus routine occurrences.

2. **Causal Chains**: Events cause other events. A discovery enables trade, trade enables cultural exchange, cultural exchange enables new technologies.

3. **Ripple Propagation**: Effects spread through space and time. A war nearby affects you immediately; a war far away affects you after delay.

4. **Narrative Depth**: The same event is told from multiple perspectives (victor, loser, neutral observer, future archaeologist), creating rich, contradictory histories.

## Tips for Exploration

### Finding Interesting Stories

1. **Start with the Timeline**: Get a visual overview of major events
2. **Look for Crisis Cascades**: Watch how one event triggers a chain of consequences
3. **Follow a Civilization**: Pick one species and trace its entire history
4. **Explore Genealogies**: See how leadership and traits pass through generations
5. **Read Multiple Perspectives**: The same event told differently reveals hidden truths

### Understanding Significance

Events are color-coded by significance:

- **Red**: Extinction events, major wars, civilization-defining moments
- **Orange**: Technological breakthroughs, first contacts, major migrations
- **Yellow**: Important but local events, leadership changes, cultural shifts
- **Blue**: Routine events, trade routes, minor discoveries

### Searching and Filtering

Use the search and filter tools to find:
- Events involving specific civilizations
- Events of specific types (war, discovery, etc.)
- Events in specific time periods
- Events affecting specific figures

## Advanced Features

### Causal Chain Viewer

Click on any event to see:
- **What caused this event?** (trace backwards through causes)
- **What did this event cause?** (trace forward through effects)
- **Who was involved?** (see all actors and their roles)
- **What changed?** (see immediate and long-term consequences)

### Genealogy Trees

Explore family lineages showing:
- Leadership succession
- Trait inheritance (aquatic adaptations, intelligence, etc.)
- Achievements and their compound effects
- Influence metrics showing who mattered most

### Figure Profiles

Click on any historical figure to see:
- Their biography and major achievements
- Their relationships with other figures
- Their influence on events
- Their descendants and legacy

## Generating Multiple Galaxies

Each galaxy is unique even with the same parameters. Try:

1. **Same preset, different name**: Generates a completely different history
2. **Different presets, same theme**: Compare how different parameters affect storytelling
3. **Seeded generation**: Use the same seed to reproduce identical galaxies (advanced feature)

## Understanding Narrative Depth

The "Narrative Depth" setting affects how detailed the generated histories are:

- **Light**: Faster generation, broader overview, less detail
- **Medium**: Balanced speed and depth, good for most users
- **Deep**: Slower generation, rich character development, detailed interconnections

## Troubleshooting

### Galaxy Generation Takes Too Long

- Try a smaller preset (Quick Start instead of Vast Cosmos)
- Reduce narrative depth to "Light" or "Medium"
- Check your internet connection

### Events Seem Disconnected

- This might be intentional! Not all events are directly connected
- Use the Causal Chain Viewer to find hidden connections
- Some civilizations are far apart and take time to influence each other

### Can't Find a Specific Event

- Use the search bar with keywords
- Filter by civilization or time period
- Check the Timeline view for visual browsing

## Philosophy

The Galaxy Simulator is built on a simple philosophy: **depth of interconnection matters far more than breadth of content**. A galaxy with 3 deeply interconnected civilizations generating genuinely surprising historical narratives is infinitely more valuable than 100 isolated civilizations with shallow histories.

Each galaxy is a unique exploration of how systems evolve, how small events cascade into major consequences, and how civilizations adapt to challenges. Enjoy your exploration!
